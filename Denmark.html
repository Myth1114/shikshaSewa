Why study in Denmark?

Denmark offers a world-class education system with a strong emphasis on research, innovation, and sustainability. The universities collaborate closely with industries, ensuring that students gain practical experience alongside their studies. Courses are often taught in English, making it accessible to international students. Additionally, Denmark provides free education for EU/EEA students, while non-EU students can benefit from scholarships and grants. The high quality of life, excellent public services, and welcoming atmosphere make Denmark a top choice for international students.

Education in Denmark

The Danish education system follows the Bologna Process, ensuring internationally recognized degrees Many programs, especially at the master's level, are offered in English, attracting students from around the world. The degree structure is:

  • Bachelor's Degree: 3 years
  • Master's Degree: 2 years
  • PhD: 3-4 years

Student Life in Denmark

Student life in Denmark is a unique blend of academics, cultural experiences, and social engagement. Universities emphasize interactive learning, where students participate in group projects, case studies, and research-driven discussions. Danish campuses are modern and well-equipped, offering libraries, student centers, and recreational facilities. International students find Denmark welcoming and inclusive, with many cultural events, student associations, and networking opportunities. The concept of "hygge", meaning coziness and well-being, is deeply embedded in Danish society, making social life enjoyable. Denmark's bicycle-friendly cities, green spaces, and excellent public transport allow students to explore freely. Part-time work opportunities help students gain professional experience while studying. Overall, Denmark offers a safe, vibrant, and enriching student life, balancing education with leisure and personal growth.

Student Visa Requirement For Denmark

International students from outside the EU/EEA need a Residence Permit (Student Visa) to study in Denmark. Requirements include:

Requirements:

  • An admission letter from a Danish university
  • Proof of financial resources (around DKK 6,397 per month for living expenses)
  • Proof of health insurance.
  • A valid passport
  • Visa processing can take up to 2 months, so students should apply early.

Once in Denmark, students must register with the Danish Civil Registration System (CPR) to access healthcare and public services.

Post-Study Work (PSW) in the Denmark

Denmark offers excellent post-study work opportunities for international graduates, allowing them to transition smoothly from academia to professional careers. Non-EU/EEA students can apply for a job-seeking visa that grants them up to three years to find employment after graduation. Denmark's Positive List, which highlights occupations in high demand, makes it easier for skilled graduates to secure jobs in fields like engineering, IT, healthcare, business, and life sciences. Many companies actively recruit international graduates, and internships or student jobs during studies often lead to full-time employment. Graduates who find a job can apply for a work and residence permit, enabling them to stay long-term. With its high wages, strong labor market, and balanced work culture, Denmark provides an attractive post-study work environment for ambitious professionals looking to build a career in Europe.

Tuition Fees in Denmark

Approximate annual tuition fees for international students.

  • EU/EEA Students: Free education
  • Non-EU Students: Tuition fees range from €6,000 - €16,000 per year, depending on the program and university
  • Engineering, technology, and business programs usually have higher fees

These figures are approximate and can vary significantly between institutions.

Cost of Living in Denmark

Denmark is known for its high cost of living, but students can manage expenses with careful planning:

  • Accommodation: €400 - €800 per month
  • Food & Groceries: €200 - €300 per month
  • Transport: €40 - €60 per month (bicycles and student discounts on public transport help save costs)
  • Total Estimated Cost: €800 - €1,500 per month, depending on the city Students often opt for shared apartments or university housing to lower costs.
